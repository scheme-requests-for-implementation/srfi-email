Even though I think it's a questionable feature, the fact that popular Scheme implementations already implement #; is a strong argument in favor of standardizing it with a SRFI. The SRFI only mentions this is passing, in "This syntax is already in wide use". I suggest mentioning the "existing art" more prominently in the Rationale, perhaps listing known implementations that provide the feature.
