> Seesh. Would you guys take the much more awkward clause "one cannot
> implement classes or units where variables bound by fields in classes
> or imports/definitions in units are not wrapped in parenthesis"?

Sure.  And that's in the spirit of Scheme, where a call may do anything,
but a mere variable reference may not.  (Syntactic keywords aren't
really an exception, because the list of keywords is short and
very specifically per-macro.)
